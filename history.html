History of the Arboretum

From a Victorian vision to a beloved community green space — over 180 years of history.

The Beginning

Britain's First Public Park

Derby Arboretum holds a remarkable distinction: it was the first park in Britain to be given freely to the public. Opened on 16 September 1840, the park was a gift to the people of Derby from local mill owner Joseph Strutt.

Strutt commissioned the celebrated landscape designer John Claudius Loudon to design the park — one of the last major works of Loudon's career. Loudon's design was innovative, featuring serpentine paths, raised mounds to maximise the display of trees, and a rich collection of specimen trees from around the world.

The opening was a grand civic occasion, attended by thousands of Derby residents and celebrated with music, processions, and festivities. It set a precedent for public parks throughout Britain and the world.
